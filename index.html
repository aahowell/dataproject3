The world's banana problem: 5 questions you were too embarrassed to ask

Updated by Aleah Howell on March 28, 2016

What banana problem?

Banana plantations are being hit by a fungal disease called Tropical Race 4, a strain of Panama disease. With no banana resistant to the strain, the United Nations says the disease threatens global supply of one of the world's top food crops. Top banana exporters, Ecuador, Philippines and other Latin American countries have yet to be hit by the disease and are taking steps to limit the risk. However, while prevention measures will stall the arrival of Race 4, scientists are saying it is inevitable that the disease spreads to all producing countries.

Where do my bananas come from?

The majority of the United States' and Europe's bananas come from Latin America. Only 15 percent of the world's bananas are grown primarily for export. Countries that grow the most bananas keep them for domestic consumption.

Are my bananas more expensive now?

Yes and no. Price of the banana have gradually increased over time, but not necessarily as a result of the Panama Disease. Since the United States gets bananas from Latin America and the Caribbean, where Panama Disease has yet to hit, prices of bananas have not dramatically increased.
